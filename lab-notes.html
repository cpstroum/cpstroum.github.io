SO-101: teaching a cheap robot arm to do useful things

Field Notes are the writing; this is the workbench. Lab Notes tracks the experiments I'm running on my SO-101 arm — imitation learning from demonstrations, the datasets and policies that come out of it, and the sim and real-world runs in between. Progress, dead ends, and the infographic that ties it together, all in one place.

What's next

Open threads on the bench right now.

In progress
Sim-to-real for episode efficiency. Using simulation to see how far the required demonstration count can drop before real-world performance degrades — comparing policies trained on fewer real episodes plus sim augmentation against the real-only baseline.
Planned
Migrate to Gr00t N1.7. Swap the current policy backbone for NVIDIA's Gr00t N1.7 foundation model and compare sample efficiency and rollout quality against the from-scratch policy.
Exploring
Broader task set. Beyond the current pick-and-place task, testing whether the same data pipeline generalizes to new object types and manipulation tasks without a full re-collection.
